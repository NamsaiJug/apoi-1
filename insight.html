Asia Parliamentary Openness Index

About the Index Methodology

This cycle's assessment finds wide variation in how openly the region's parliaments operate. A handful of unicameral chambers — Taiwan, Australia, and South Korea — lead the pack with strong disclosure of proceedings and reasonably accessible records, while several bicameral upper houses lag noticeably behind their own lower chambers. Transparency around meeting agendas and broadcasts is comparatively strong across the board, but accountability — asset declarations, conflict-of-interest disclosure, oversight of publicly funded staff — is the weakest dimension almost everywhere, and no parliament yet fully achieves every citizen participation indicator.
